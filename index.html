Introduction to Atmospheric Pollution.

  • I want to start by first defining what is meant by atmospheric pollution. Atmospheric pollution refers to any gas or particulate matter at a high concentration which may be harmful to life or the environment. A pollutant may originate from natural or anthropogenic sources or both. Studies of WHO( World Health Organization ) reveals that seven million people die annually due to exposure to atmospheric pollution. In India, about 1.25 million people die annually due to atmospheric pollution. Air pollution is the 6th most deadly killer in Southeast Asia and the 5th largest killer in India

While researching for my assignment I found this amazing site on the internet which I would like to refer to. It shows the real-time ranking of most air-polluted cities in the world - World Air Quality Index (AQI) Ranking | AirVisual

Air Quality Index (AQI)

It is a numerical scale used to measure and report the air quality of an area on a given day. Eight pollutants namely PM10, PM2.5, NO2, SO2, CO, O3, NH3, and Pb are the major parameters taken into consideration while deriving the AQI of a region

India's Rankings

In a survey conducted in 2019, India was ranked 6th most air polluted country in the world with 6 out of 10 of the world’s most air-polluted cities coming from India

Most Polluted Cities

this include Ghaziabad, Delhi, Noida, Gurugram, Greater Noida, Bandhwari. Pollution is the greatest risk to human life, more so in India than in any other country.

Health Affects

Adverse effects on human life and health

Lung Diseases and Respiratory Problems

Conditions such as asthma and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) can be caused or triggered by rising levels of air pollution.

Lung Cancer

Indoor air pollution can lead to Lung cancer including adhesive and chemicals used in furniture can release formaldehyde fumes, which are a known cause for cancer.

Cardiovascular Problems

Breathing in polluted air for long periods can increase the risk of heart diseases. Harmful air pollutants such as carbon monoxide, nitrogen dioxide, and ozone can get mixed in the bloodstream and cause cardiovascular disorders

Affects Pregnant Women and New Borns

Indoor air pollution can also have adverse effects on the health of women during pregnancy. The unborn child can suffer from a weaker immune system and may have a lung disorder upon birth.

Impact Of Lockdown On Air Quality In India

The lockdown showed some pronounced improvement in air quality even in large densely populated cities in India. Unfortunately on the one hand lives of hundreds of millions of Indian people have been disrupted due to the lockdown in response to the COVID-19 pandemic but the Covid also brought some good results in terms of a decline in air pollutants even in the cities like Delhi and Kolkata which are known to be highly polluted cities in India and the world. These results should be taken as learning by the Indian Government to ponder on how to strictly minimize vehicular and industrial pollution to improve air quality which will help to sustain better public health in India
